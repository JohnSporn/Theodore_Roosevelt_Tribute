Theodore Roosevelt

The Rough Rider


Timeline of Theodore Roosevelts life:


  • 1858 - Theodore Roosevelt is born at 28 East 20th Street, New York, NY
  • 1876 - Attends Harvard University studying history and government
  • 1880 - Marries Alice Hathaway Lee
  • 1881 - Elected to New York State Assembly at the age of 23
  • 1884 - Wife Alice and mother die, only hours apart. Moves to the Dakota Territories
  • 1886 - Marries childhood friend Edith Kermit Carow
  • 1889 - Starts as U.S. Civil Service Commissioner in Washington, DC
  • 1895 - Becomes Police Commissioner of New York City and is later elected President of the Board of Police Commissioners. Receives national attention for police reforms in New York City
  • 1897 - Appointed Assistant Secretary of the Navy
  • 1898 - Becomes a war hero after leading a regiment of calvary known as "Rough Riders" in Cuba during the Spanish-American War. Then in November is elected Governor of New York
  • 1900 - Elected U.S. vice-president under President William McKinley
  • 1901 - Roosevelt becomes the youngest U.S. president after the assassination of William McKinley
  • 1902 - Establishes Crater Lake National Park, the first of five parks founded during his presidency.
  • 1904 - Issues "Roosevelt Corollary" to Monroe Doctrine, arguing that the U.S. can act as "an international police power" and intervene in Latin America
  • 1905 - Establishes National Forest Service
  • 1906 - Awarded Nobel Peace Prize for facilitating negotiations that end Russo-Japanese War
  • 1907 - Signs Immigration Act of 1907, which allows president to restrict number of immigrants from Japan
  • 1909 - Presidency ends; Roosevelt and his son Kermit spend a year on safari with the Smithsonian-Roosevelt African Expedition
  • 1912 - Nominated as presidential candidate of Progressive party (Bull Moose party). Shot in the chest before campaign speech. Finishes the speech before receiving medical attention
  • 1914 - Goes on expedition to explore an uncharted tibutary of Amazon River. Injures leg severely during expedition aand nearly dies
  • 1918 - Publishes "The Great Adventure"
  • 1919 - Dies in sleep at Sagamore Hill of a coronary embolism at age 60
"Speak softly and carry a big stick; you will go far" -Theodore Roosevelt
